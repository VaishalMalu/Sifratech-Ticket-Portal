1. Customer Guide

Welcome to the Sifratech Support experience. We have designed our portal to make raising and tracking incidents as frictionless as possible. You can raise a ticket directly from your Microsoft Outlook client, or log into the portal for full visibility.

1.1 Raising a Ticket via Outlook

You do not need to log into the portal to raise a ticket. Simply send an email to our dedicated support address.

1

Draft your Email

Open Outlook and create a new email addressed to asmsupport@sifratc.com.

2

Choose your format (Plain vs Template)

Plain Email: You can simply type your issue in plain text. Our backend AI will automatically read your email, determine the urgency, categorize the Oracle Module, and assign it to the right team.

Structured Template: If you want to ensure 100% accuracy and bypass AI guessing, you can use our standardized template. (Highly Recommended for complex technical issues).

1.2 The Official Email Template

If you prefer to provide structured data that our system can instantly map to database fields, copy and paste the below block into your email body. Fill in the values after the colons.

To:
asmsupport@sifratc.com
Subject:
[Your Issue Summary]
**SIFRATECH TICKET TEMPLATE** (Please fill the details below) Priority: High / Medium / Low Module: Financials / HRMS / SCM / PPM / Payroll Type: Bug / Data Fix / New Requirement Phone: +971 xx xxx xxxx Ext: 1234 **Description of Issue:** Please describe the issue in detail here...

AI Triage Feature

If you don't use the template, don't worry! Our integrated Anthropic Claude AI model will read your plain text email and automatically extract the priority, module, and intent.

1.3 Accessing the Client Portal

Once your email is sent, you will immediately receive an automated confirmation email containing your Ticket ID (e.g., TKT-2026-104592) and a secure link to the portal.

1

Login Securely

Click the link in your email or navigate to support.sifratech.com. Enter your registered email address and your password. Access is strictly authenticated via our secure backend.

2

Track Status

On the "Tickets" tab, you will see a live view of your tickets. You can see whether the ticket is Open, Assigned, In Progress, or Resolved.

3

Communicate with Engineers

Click on any ticket to open the details view. You can add comments, attach screenshots/logs, and view updates directly from the Sifratech engineering team.

2. Support Team Guide (Internal)

This section is for Sifratech Support Engineers. The portal provides a focused, zero-clutter environment to manage your assigned workloads effectively.

2.1 Navigating the Dashboard

When you log in, the system recognizes your role as an Engineer and filters your view.

Personalized Dashboard

Your KPI metrics only reflect tickets assigned to you or your specific module group. You won't be distracted by unrelated data.

SLA Tracking

Tickets nearing their SLA breach limit are automatically highlighted in red. Focus on the 'Ageing' metrics to prioritize your day.

2.2 Managing & Resolving Tickets

Handling a ticket requires adherence to strict status workflows to ensure clients receive accurate automated updates.

  1. Acknowledge: When a new ticket is assigned to you, open it and add an initial comment. This automatically shifts the status to In Progress and emails the client.
  2. Investigate: Use the AI Summary panel. The AI pre-reads long email chains and gives you a 2-sentence summary, the client's sentiment (e.g., Frustrated, Neutral), and recommended next steps.
  3. Communicate: If you need more info, change the status to Awaiting Customer.
  4. Resolve: Once fixed, use the Resolution Notes box. You can even click the ✨ AI Reply Suggestion button to have the AI generate a professional resolution email based on your technical notes.

Role Level Security (RLS)

You cannot close tickets that do not belong to your team, and you cannot view sensitive Account Management settings. Access is enforced at the database level.

3. Account Manager Guide

As an Account Manager or Delivery Manager, you have elevated "See All" privileges. Your goal is operational oversight, ensuring SLAs are met across all clients and modules.

3.1 Operational Overview & KPIs

Your dashboard aggregates data across the entire organization. You will see macro-level donuts and bar charts breaking down incident volume by Oracle Module, Priority, and Status.

  • SLA Monitoring: A dedicated chart shows SLA compliance percentages across Top, High, Medium, and Low priorities.
  • Team Workload: Navigate to the Team tab to see a real-time matrix of all engineers, how many open tickets they hold, and their average resolution times.

3.2 Manual Assignment & Escalations

While the AI and Webhook system attempts to auto-assign tickets based on the seed_users.js team mappings, you retain ultimate control.

1

Review Unassigned Tickets

Filter the main ticket list by "Unassigned". Open the ticket details.

2

Select Engineer

Use the "Ticket Assignment" dropdown visible only to Managers. Select the appropriate engineer from the live database list and click Assign Engineer.

3

Automated Notifications

The system will instantly dispatch a secure email via Microsoft Graph API to the engineer notifying them of the assignment, and a parallel email to the client confirming their assigned handler.

4. Developer & Technical Architecture

This section outlines what was built under the hood to deliver this production-ready enterprise application.

4.1 Stack & Infrastructure

React 18 Vite Node.js Express Supabase (PostgreSQL) Microsoft Graph API Anthropic Claude AI

Frontend Architecture (Sifratech Portal React)

  • Routing & Protection: Utilizes react-router-dom with a strict <ProtectedRoute> wrapper. Unauthenticated users are blocked.
  • State Management: Built with modular React Contexts (AuthContext, DataContext, ModalContext) to prevent prop-drilling and ensure clean separation of concerns.
  • Realtime: Supabase realtime subscriptions (postgres_changes) are implemented on the tickets table, allowing the UI to update instantly without refreshing when an email arrives.

Backend Architecture (Sifratech Backend)

  • Authentication: Fully integrated with Supabase Auth. Credentials are secure, and session tokens are validated on every secure route.
  • Database schema: Relational structure handling users, roles, teams, tickets, ticket_comments, and ticket_status_history.
  • RLS (Row Level Security): Postgres policies restrict data access at the database level ensuring Clients only see their company's tickets.

4.2 Integration Specifications

Microsoft Graph API (Email Processing)

The Node.js backend exposes a webhook /api/webhooks/outlook. When an email hits asmsupport@sifratc.com:

  1. Microsoft posts the payload to the backend.
  2. The emailProcessor.js service parses the HTML body.
  3. It checks if the email matches the strict Regex Template. If it does, it maps it directly.
  4. If it's plain text, it passes the text to the AI for extraction.
  5. A ticket is inserted into Supabase, and replies are dispatched via the Graph API /sendMail endpoint.

AI Triage & Sentiment Analysis

The portal utilizes LLMs (Large Language Models) in two distinct ways:

  • Backend Extraction: Turning unstructured angry customer emails into structured JSON (Priority, Module).
  • Frontend Assistance: The /api/ai/suggest-reply endpoint allows engineers to click a button and generate a perfectly formatted resolution response based on brief, shorthand technical notes.

Security Notice Executed

During final delivery, all legacy local-storage demo fallback accounts were stripped from the codebase. The platform now relies 100% on cryptographically signed JWTs provided by Supabase Auth.
