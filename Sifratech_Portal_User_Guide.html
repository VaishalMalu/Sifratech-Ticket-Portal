Portal Overview & Key Features

What the Sifratech Support Portal does and how it serves you

The Sifratech Support Portal is a dedicated, enterprise-grade help desk platform that manages all support interactions between your organisation and the Sifratech team. Every request, question, bug report, or change requirement is logged as a structured ticket, ensuring nothing is missed, every commitment is tracked, and all communication is documented in one central location.

100%

Transparent — every action is logged with a full audit trail

24/7

Always accessible — raise tickets at any time from any browser

Live

Real-time dashboard — your ticket data updates instantly

What You Can Do in the Portal

  • Raise support tickets — log any issue, bug, data request, enhancement, or training need directly through the portal form.
  • Track progress in real time — see exactly who is working on your ticket, what status it is in, and how long it has been open.
  • Communicate with the support team — add comments directly on a ticket and attach screenshots, log files, or supporting documents.
  • Review your history — access all past and present tickets for your organisation in the Tickets section.
  • Monitor SLA compliance — the dashboard clearly shows whether tickets are being resolved within the agreed timeframes.
  • Receive automated email updates — get notified by email whenever a ticket is created, assigned, updated, or resolved without needing to log in.
  • Accept or reopen resolutions — when a ticket is marked Resolved, you may close it formally or reopen it if the issue persists.

Supported Modules

The portal is pre-configured to handle tickets across the following functional areas of your ERP system:

Financials   •   HRMS   •   SCM   •   PPM   •   Sourcing   •   Inventory   •   Payroll   •   Other

Portal Access & Login Credentials

How to access the portal and sign in for the first time

Step 1 — Open the Portal

Open any modern web browser (Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, or Mozilla Firefox are recommended) and navigate to the portal URL provided by your Sifratech account manager. No software installation is required.

Portal URL

The portal URL will be shared with you directly by your Sifratech account manager via email. Please bookmark the address once you have received it.

Step 2 — Enter Your Credentials

On the login screen, enter your assigned username and password. These credentials are unique to you and should not be shared with other individuals.

Note on Credentials

For security purposes, individual login credentials are not distributed in this document. Your account credentials will be securely shared via the official team group. Please refer to the designated internal communication channels to retrieve your secure login details.

Step 3 — Sign In

Click the Sign in button or press Enter. The system will validate your credentials and redirect you to the Dashboard. If your credentials are incorrect, an error message will appear on screen.

Security Notice

The portal displays "Authorised Access Only" on the login screen. Access is restricted to accounts provisioned by Sifratech. If you believe you should have access but cannot log in, contact your Sifratech account manager.

Step 4 — Logging Out

Always sign out when you have finished using the portal, particularly on shared or public computers. Click the sign-out icon located in the top-right corner of the navigation bar to securely end your session.

User Roles & Permissions

Understanding what each role can access and perform within the portal

The portal operates on a Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) model. Each user is assigned a role that determines what they can see and what actions they can perform. There are four primary roles:

Role Create Ticket View Tickets Assign Engineer Update Status Close / Reopen
Customer Yes Own org only No Close / Reopen only Yes
Support Engineer Yes All assigned No Assigned / In Progress / Resolved No
Account Manager Yes All tickets Yes Full control Yes
Administrator Yes All tickets Yes Full control Yes
What This Means for You as a Customer

As a customer user, you can create tickets freely, monitor their progress, add comments and attachments, and make the final decision to either formally close a resolved ticket or reopen it if the issue has not been fully addressed. You do not need to assign engineers — that is managed by the Sifratech team.

Creating a New Ticket — Step by Step

How to log a support request from start to submission within the portal

To raise a new support ticket, navigate to the Tickets section using the top navigation bar, then click the New Ticket button in the upper-right corner. Complete all required fields as described below.

  • Navigate to Tickets

    Click Tickets in the top navigation bar. The ticket list for your organisation will be displayed.

  • Click "New Ticket"

    Press the New Ticket button at the top right. The ticket creation form will open as an overlay panel.

  • Complete the Form

    Fill in all required fields marked with a red asterisk (*). Optional fields help provide additional context and are encouraged where applicable.

  • Review AI Suggestions (Optional)

    As you type the incident title, the system's AI assistant may suggest a priority level, module, and ticket type. You may apply these suggestions or continue with your own selections.

  • Click "Create Ticket"

    Press the Create Ticket button. Your ticket is immediately logged in the system and you will receive an email confirmation.

Ticket Form Fields

Field Required Description & Guidance
Project * Yes The name of your ERP project or system. Pre-filled as Oracle EBS R12 by default.
Incident Title * Yes A concise, descriptive title for the issue. Keep it to one clear sentence.
Incident Type * Yes Select the category that best describes your request (Bug, Data Fix, Enhancement, etc.).
Module * Yes The functional area of the system affected (e.g. Financials, HRMS, Payroll).
Priority Optional Your assessment of the urgency. Defaults to Medium.
Environment Optional The system environment where the issue was found (Production, UAT, Test).
Long Description * Yes A detailed description of the issue. Include steps to reproduce, error messages, and expected behaviour.

Creating Tickets via Email (Outlook Integration)

Complete End-User Process for Email Submission

For maximum convenience, you can log, track, and manage support tickets entirely through your email client (like Microsoft Outlook) without needing to log into the web portal.

Official Support Address

asmsupport@sifratc.com

Phase 1: Initiating the Request

1. Open Microsoft Outlook (or your preferred email client) and click New Email.

2. In the To: field, enter the official support address: asmsupport@sifratc.com.

3. In the Subject: field, write a clear and concise summary following this exact format: [Module Name] - [Brief Issue Summary].

Phase 2: Applying the Required Template

You must copy the official template below and paste it into the body of your email to ensure all required fields are captured.

To: asmsupport@sifratc.com Subject: [Module Name] - [Brief Issue Summary] -------------------------------------------------- Dear Support Team, Please find the incident details below. ### Incident Details Incident Name: Incident Description: Oracle Module: (Financials / SCM / Payroll / HRMS / Procurement / Inventory / Projects / Other) Environment: (Production / UAT / Test / Development) Priority: (Top / High / Medium / Low / Project) Business Impact: Affected Users: Error Message / Error Code: Date & Time of Occurrence: Recent Changes (if any): Steps to Reproduce: 1. 2. 3. Expected Result: Actual Result: --------------------------------------------------

Phase 3: Submission & Tracking

Attach any relevant screenshots directly to the email and click Send. Within minutes, you will receive an automated confirmation email containing your unique Ticket ID. You can reply directly to this email chain to add comments or communicate with the assigned support engineer.

Sample Ticket — Filled Example

A realistic example of a correctly completed ticket submission

The following example illustrates how a well-structured ticket should look when submitted through the portal or via email. Use this as a reference for your own submissions.

TKT-2026-0042

Invoice approval workflow not triggering email notifications to approvers

Open High
ProjectOracle EBS R12
ModuleFinancials
Incident TypeBug
EnvironmentProduction

Since the morning of 28 June 2026, the AP invoice approval workflow in Oracle EBS R12 (Production) is not sending email notifications to designated approvers when invoices are submitted for approval.

Steps to Reproduce:
1. Log in to Oracle EBS as an AP Invoice entry user.
2. Enter a new AP invoice and submit it for approval.
3. The invoice status changes to "Pending Approval" correctly in the system.
4. However, the designated first-level approver does not receive the standard approval notification email.

Expected Behaviour: The approver should receive an automated email notification with the invoice details and an approval link.

Actual Behaviour: No email is received. The approver only discovers pending invoices by manually logging in and checking their worklist.

Business Impact: Invoice approvals are delayed, causing payment processing to stall. Approximately 15 invoices are currently pending unnoticed.

Email Notifications — What to Expect

Automated emails you will receive at each stage of your ticket's journey

The portal automatically sends email notifications at critical points in the ticket lifecycle. You do not need to stay logged in to the portal to receive updates. All notification emails originate from the official portal system.

Email 1 — Ticket Created

Sent immediately upon ticket submission to confirm your request has been logged. It contains your unique Ticket Number, priority, and SLA commitment time.

Email 2 — Ticket In Progress

Sent when a support engineer begins actively working on your ticket and posts their first update or asks for clarification.

Email 3 — Ticket Resolved

Sent when the support team has completed work and marked the ticket as Resolved. You are required to verify the resolution on your end and either Close the ticket or Reopen it if the issue persists.

Summary of Notification Triggers

Trigger Event Email Sent To Purpose
Ticket Created Ticket raiser + CC Confirms the request has been logged and provides ticket reference.
Ticket Assigned Assigned engineer Notifies the internal engineer that a ticket has been allocated to them.
Ticket In Progress Ticket raiser Confirms the team has begun active investigation.
Ticket Resolved Ticket raiser + CC Delivers the resolution summary and requests customer confirmation.

The Complete Lifecycle: End-to-End Internal & External Process

  • 1. Customer Initiation

    The Customer creates a ticket via the portal form or by sending an email via Outlook using the official Incident Reporting Template.

  • 2. Ticket Creation & Confirmation

    The system automatically generates a new ticket and sends a "Ticket Created" confirmation email back to the customer.

  • 3. Account Manager Reception

    The internal Account Manager receives the newly created incident in their dashboard for initial review.

  • 4. Triage & Assignment

    The Account Manager evaluates the details and clicks Assign to route the ticket to the specific support agent responsible for that area.

  • 5. Agent Notification & Investigation

    An automated notification is sent to the assigned support person. The agent logs into the portal and utilizes the built-in AI Summarizer feature to rapidly digest the lengthy incident description.

  • 6. Resolution & Customer Notification

    After completing the work, the agent clicks Resolved. A resolution email is sent directly to the customer informing them the issue is fixed.

  • 7. Verification & Final Closure

    The customer verifies the fix. If satisfied, the Account Manager marks the ticket for Closure, completing the process end-to-end.

Service Level Agreement (SLA) Commitments

Response and resolution timeframes for each priority level

Every ticket submitted through the portal is automatically assigned a Service Level Agreement (SLA) target based on its priority level. The SLA defines the maximum number of hours within which the Sifratech team commits to respond and provide a resolution.

SLA Targets by Priority

Top4Hours
High8Hours
Medium24Hours
Low72Hours
Project120Hours

Priority Level Definitions

Priority Definition Examples
Top A critical system failure that is completely preventing business operations. The system is down or core financial processing is halted. System inaccessible for all users; payroll unable to process; financial period close blocked.
High A significant defect that is severely impacting daily operations. A workaround may exist but is unsatisfactory. Invoice approval emails not being sent; reports producing incorrect figures.
Medium An issue that affects functionality but does not prevent core business operations. A reasonable workaround is available. A non-critical field displaying incorrect data; minor UI display error; single user access issue.
Low A minor issue, cosmetic defect, or general query with no significant operational impact. Label alignment issue; request for general guidance; non-urgent enhancement query.
SLA Breach Monitoring

The portal dashboard prominently displays any tickets that have exceeded their SLA target. If your ticket has breached its SLA, the Sifratech management team is automatically alerted and will escalate the matter internally.

The portal incorporates an integrated AI assistant designed to enhance the speed and accuracy of support:

Frequently Asked Questions

I submitted a ticket but have not received a confirmation email. What should I do?
Check your spam or junk folder first. If the ticket is visible in your portal dashboard, it has been successfully submitted.

Can I update or edit a ticket after it has been submitted?
Ticket fields cannot be edited after submission to preserve the audit trail. However, you can add additional information by posting a comment on the ticket.

What happens if I receive an "Awaiting Customer" status?
This means the support team requires additional information from you before they can continue working. Log into the portal and respond via comment.

My password has expired or I cannot remember it. How do I reset it?
Password resets are handled by the Sifratech administrator. Contact support from your registered email address to request a reset.
